Understanding Percentages

What is a percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "per hundred." It's denoted by the symbol "%".

Example: 25% = 25/100 = 0.25

How do you convert between percentages, decimals, and fractions?

Percentage to decimal: Divide by 100 (or move decimal point 2 places left)
Decimal to percentage: Multiply by 100 (or move decimal point 2 places right)
Percentage to fraction: Put the percentage over 100 and simplify

75% = 75/100 = 0.75 = 3/4
0.6 = 60% = 60/100 = 3/5

What are some common percentage calculations in daily life?

Sales tax: If sales tax is 8.5%, and you buy something for $50, the tax is $4.25
Tips: A 20% tip on a $30 meal is $6
Discounts: A 30% discount on a $80 item saves you $24
Interest rates: 5% annual interest on $1000 is $50 per year

How do you calculate percentage increase and decrease?

Formula: ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100

Price went from $100 to $120:
Increase = ((120 - 100) / 100) × 100 = 20%

Price dropped from $200 to $150:
Decrease = ((150 - 200) / 200) × 100 = -25%

What's the difference between percentage points and percent?

Percentage points refer to the arithmetic difference between percentages. If unemployment rises from 5% to 7%, that's an increase of 2 percentage points, but a 40% relative increase.

Interest rate: 3% → 5%
Percentage point increase: 2 points
Percent increase: 66.7%

How are percentages used in finance and business?

Profit margins: (Revenue - Costs) / Revenue × 100
Growth rates: Year-over-year percentage changes
Return on investment (ROI): (Gain - Cost) / Cost × 100
Market share: Company sales / Total market sales × 100
